Fish and Chips

Servings: 4 Prepping Time: 15 min Cooking Time: 20 min

Ingredients

    For the Fish:

  • 7 tablespoons (55g) all-purpose flour, divided
  • 7 tablespoons (55g) cornstarch
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • Fine salt, to taste
  • Freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  • 1/3 cup cold dark beer
  • 1/3 cup cold sparkling water
  • 4 (7-ounce) thick white fish fillets

    • For the Chips:

  • 2 pounds (about 900g) potatoes, peeled
  • Vegetable oil, for deep frying

Instructions

  1. Prepare the batter:

    2. In a bowl, mix 6 tablespoons of the flour with the cornstarch, baking powder, salt, and pepper. Gradually whisk in the cold beer and sparkling water until you get a smooth batter. Let the batter rest while you prepare the chips.

  2. Prepare the chips:

    3. Cut the peeled potatoes into thick fries. Rinse them in cold water to remove excess starch, then pat dry with a paper towel.

  3. Pre-fry the chips:

    4. Heat oil in a deep fryer or large pan to 320°F (160°C). Fry the potatoes for about 5 minutes until they are soft but not browned. Remove and set aside on a paper towel.

  4. Fry the fish:

    5. Heat the oil to 375°F (190°C). Lightly coat the fish fillets with the remaining 1 tablespoon of flour, then dip them into the batter. Fry until golden brown and crispy, about 5-7 minutes. Remove and let drain on a paper towel.

  5. Finish the chips:

    6. Increase the oil temperature back to 375°F (190°C). Fry the pre-cooked potatoes again for 3-4 minutes until crispy and golden. Drain on a paper towel and season with salt.

  6. Serve:

    7. Serve the crispy fish fillets with the golden fries. Optionally, add tartar sauce, malt vinegar, or a lemon wedge for extra flavor.

Tips & Variations

Use cod, haddock, or pollock for the best texture.
Keeping the beer and sparkling water cold makes the batter crispier.
Double frying the chips ensures they are crispy on the outside and fluffy on the inside.
